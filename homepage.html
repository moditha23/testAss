Ceylon Tea

Ceylon Tea, introduced in 1867, has grown to be the top agricultural export in Sri Lanka, providing direct and indirect employment to nearly 1 million people, while tea plantations cover approximately 4% of the country's land area, amounting to nearly 203000 hectares. Sri Lanka is a tea-growing island.

Quality Is Key

Over the course of 150 years, the name Ceylon had become synonymous with the finest tea in the world. Ceylon was tea in the eyes and tongues of the world, and tea was Ceylon.Ceylon is to tea what Rolls Royce is to cars, Rolex is to watches, .To protect this commitment - the symbol of quality - the legendary lion of the Sri Lankan flag was introduced to the Ceylon Tea logo..

Classy Flavours

Flavoured Ceylon Tea, an infusion of Ceylon Tea and Sri Lankan fruits, herbs, and spices, is available in a variety of forms, flavors, and blends around the world. Since the third century AD, various blends and types of tea have been mixed with herbs, spices, and fruits to enhance the flavor and aroma of tea. Keeping with this age-old tradition, Ceylon Tea manufacturers have developed a variety of tea flavors using various flavoring methods.

Best Cup of Tea

Nothing beats watching the rolling boil of hot water as it is gently poured over fragrant tea leaves. The view is breathtaking, and it brings back memories of calm, peaceful serenity for many of us. But what goes into making a good cup of tea? It depends on skill as much as it does on the time of day, type of tea, and type of person.
